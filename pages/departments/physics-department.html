Department of Physics

Department of Physics

The Department of Physics at Nalanda University is dedicated to exploring the fundamental laws of nature, from quantum mechanics to astrophysics. Our state-of-the-art laboratories and distinguished faculty provide students with an exceptional environment for learning and research in physics.

Our Mission

To advance the understanding of physical phenomena through rigorous research and to educate the next generation of physicists who will contribute to scientific advancement and technological innovation.

Programs Offered

Bachelor of Science in Physics

A four-year undergraduate program providing a strong foundation in classical and modern physics, with opportunities for specialization in various subfields.

  • Duration: 4 years
  • Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics and Mathematics
  • Intake: 60 students per year
View Syllabus

Master of Science in Physics

An advanced two-year program offering specialized training in theoretical and experimental physics, preparing students for research careers or doctoral studies.

  • Duration: 2 years
  • Eligibility: Bachelor's degree in Physics or related field
  • Intake: 30 students per year
View Syllabus

Ph.D. in Physics

A research-focused doctoral program for students pursuing original contributions to physics, with opportunities to work alongside leading researchers in the field.

  • Duration: 3-5 years
  • Eligibility: Master's degree in Physics with research experience
  • Intake: Based on research openings

Laboratories and Facilities

Our department is equipped with modern laboratories and facilities to support teaching and cutting-edge research:

Advanced Optics Laboratory

Equipped with state-of-the-art lasers, interferometers, and spectroscopic instruments for precise optical measurements and experiments.

Condensed Matter Physics Laboratory

Features cryostats, magnetic measurement systems, and thin film deposition units for studying material properties at various conditions.

High Energy Physics Laboratory

Includes particle detectors and data analysis workstations for studying fundamental particles and their interactions.

Computational Physics Laboratory

A cluster of high-performance computers for simulations and modeling of complex physical phenomena and systems.

Electronics and Instrumentation Laboratory

Provides tools for circuit design, fabrication, and measurement, supporting experimental physics research across disciplines.
